Ace Your Next Interview

Whether it's your first time or you're getting back into the workforce, we've got you covered with tips, examples, and encouragement.

What to Expect

  • Be ready to introduce yourself in a confident, positive way.
  • Most employers ask about past experience, skills, and how you handle challenges.
  • Be honest, but don't let your past define the conversation — highlight your growth, your work ethic, and what you bring to the table now.

Common Interview Questions

  • “Tell me about yourself.”
    Keep it short. Share your strengths, experience, and why you're excited to work.
  • “Why should we hire you?”
    Show your work ethic, reliability, and what makes you a strong teammate.
  • “Can you explain your gap in employment?”
    Be honest, take ownership, and show what you've learned or how you've grown.

Do's & Don'ts

✅ Do

  • Practice your answers ahead of time
  • Dress in clean, simple clothes
  • Be honest and confident
  • Follow up after the interview

❌ Don't

  • Apologize for your past
  • Talk negatively about others
  • Interrupt or rush answers
  • Leave without asking questions
