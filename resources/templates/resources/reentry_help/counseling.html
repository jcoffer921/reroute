🧠 Counseling & Mental Health Support

Emotional health is a key part of successful reentry. These resources can help with free or low‑cost counseling, substance use treatment, and crisis support in Philadelphia and nationally.

📍 Philadelphia Services

  • Philadelphia DBHIDS – City department for behavioral health and intellectual disability services.
  • Community Behavioral Health (CBH) – Managed care for Medicaid behavioral health services in Philly.
  • Mental Health America – PA Affiliates – Local screening, peer support, and referrals.
  • Institute for Community Justice (ICJ) – Reentry services with counseling and support groups.

🇺🇸 National Hotlines & Finders

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline – Call or text 988 for 24/7 free support.
  • FindTreatment.gov (SAMHSA) – Locator for mental health and substance use treatment.
  • SAMHSA Helpline – 1‑800‑662‑HELP (4357) for treatment referral and information.
  • NAMI HelpLine – Education and support (M–F).

💡 Tips for Getting Started

  • If insured by Medicaid in Philly, call CBH to ask about covered providers.
  • Ask organizations about sliding‑scale or free counseling options.
  • Bring ID and any discharge or referral paperwork if you have it.

Last updated September 2025. Links and services can change.
